Welcome to ISKCON Raghunathpur ( শ্রী চৈতন্যচন্দ্র মন্দির )

Situated in India, West Bengal, Nadia, City: Kalyani.

ISKCON Raghunathpur is a spiritual sanctuary dedicated to spreading the teachings of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu. Our temple offers a peaceful haven for devotees and visitors, providing a space for spiritual growth and community engagement.

About Us

ISKCON Raghunathpur is a distinguished branch of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), located in the serene surroundings of West Bengal, India.

Under the esteemed leadership of President HG Bibhu Krishna Das ON Raghunathpur is committed to fostering a community grounded in the teachings of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu.

Our mission is to propagate the timeless teachings of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu, emphasizing the importance of chanting the holy names of the Lord and living a life of devotion and service.

The temple is home to the beautiful deities of Sri Jagannath Mahaprabhu, Balaram, and Subhadra. Devotees can participate in daily worship, offering their prayers and receiving the blessings of the Lord.

ISKCON Raghunathpur is deeply committed to serving the local community and beyond. We organize outreach programs, including food distribution, educational initiatives, and cultural events, to spread the message of Krishna consciousness.

Events

  • Rath Yatra: A grand chariot festival celebrating the journey of Lord Jagannath.
  • Weekly Spiritual Classes: Held every Wednesday, offering deep insights into the teachings of Srila Prabhupada.
  • Annual Janmashtami Celebration: A grand festival celebrating the appearance of Lord Krishna with cultural programs.
  • Festivals: Celebrations of major Vaishnava festivals, bringing together the community in joyous devotion.

Teachings

Books by Srila Prabhupada: Our temple library offers a wide range of books by Srila Prabhupada, providing deep insights into the philosophy of Krishna consciousness.

Offline Classes: We offer in-person classes to deepen understanding and spiritual practice.

FAQs

  • Q: What are the temple opening hours?
    A: The temple is open daily from 4:30 AM to 8:00 PM.
  • Q: Can I visit the temple if I am not a devotee?
    A: Yes, everyone is welcome to visit the temple and participate in the activities.
  • Q: Are there accommodations available for outstation visitors?
    A: Limited accommodations are available. Please contact the temple office in advance to make arrangements.
  • Q: How can I volunteer for temple activities?
    A: You can inquire at the temple office or contact us at +91 7890323187.
  • Q: How can I participate in temple festivals?
    A: Festival schedules are announced in advance. You can join us during these events or contact the temple office for more details.
